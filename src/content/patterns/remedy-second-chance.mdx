## How it works

The game first makes a desirable item artificially scarce or easy to miss, then offers — for a price — a chance to recover it. The regret of the near-miss is the lever, and the remedy itself is monetised.

## Why it can be harmful

By engineering the loss and then selling its cure, the pattern profits from manufactured regret and fear of missing out. It pressures players into spending to undo a disappointment the design deliberately created, rather than offering anything of independent value.
