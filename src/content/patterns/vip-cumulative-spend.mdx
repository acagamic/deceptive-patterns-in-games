## How it works

The game tracks a player's total spending and gates privileges, bonuses, and visible status behind cumulative thresholds. Each tier dangles the next, while time-bound "monthly card" or "growth fund" products add recurring commitments. The system is displayed openly — its persuasive force comes from making escalating spend feel like rational progression toward the next reward.

## Why it can be harmful

By rewarding gross outlay rather than engagement, it is tuned to extract maximum revenue from the highest-spending minority ("whales"), who supply a disproportionate share of free-to-play income. The endless next-tier structure encourages chasing and can entrench problematic spending, offering little protection to vulnerable or younger players. Unlike monetised social status, the lever is the spend threshold itself, not the visibility of what was bought.
