## How it works

Rather than a single wallet, the game splits spending power across many narrowly-scoped currencies — one for gacha, another for the shop, others for events or upgrades — that cannot be exchanged for one another. Players can rarely tell what anything truly costs, or consolidate what they already hold.

## Why it can be harmful

Fragmentation defeats price comparison and value intuition, and leaves stranded balances that nudge further purchases to "round up" to a usable amount. It is distinct from simple currency-conversion obfuscation: the mechanism here is partitioning funds into silos so that no single balance is ever quite enough for what the player wants.
