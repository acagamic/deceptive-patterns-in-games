Coercion shrinks the choice set rather than the player's beliefs. Non-transparent choice architecture deployed without consent — and the withholding of resources needed to progress — can itself be coercive, which maps onto engineered grind, energy gating, and obstructed exits. The structural counter-move is genuine consent and opt-out by design.
